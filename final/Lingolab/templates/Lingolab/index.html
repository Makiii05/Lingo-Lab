The Silent Crisis: Reading Literacy in the Philippines

PISA Rankings

Recent PISA results place the Philippines near the bottom in reading comprehension. The majority of 15-year-olds fall below minimum proficiency, struggling with complex texts.

The "Reading Poverty"

The World Bank highlights "learning poverty": children unable to read a simple story by age 10. Solving this requires active monitoring of fluency, speed, and comprehension.

How LingoLab Makes a Difference

LingoLab digitizes the observation process so educators can focus on learners—not paperwork.

Our system provides two core interfaces based on the manual assessment workflow:

  • Assessment Interface (Observer) Allows the evaluator (parent or teacher) to manually input reading performance metrics such as reading speed, stutter count, repeated words, and pronunciation difficulty after each session.
  • Reading Interface (Learner) The learner reads letters, syllables, or words presented on the screen, while the observer monitors the session and inputs the data for analysis.
